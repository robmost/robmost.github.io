About

I am a data scientist and computational astrophysicist who builds and analyses large-scale predictive models. My research has involved running high-resolution cosmological simulations to study alternative dark matter models, galaxy cluster physics, and large-scale structures. A large part of my day-to-day involves digging into the numerical quirks of these simulations, using statistical models to figure out what is a real physical effect and what is just a computational artefact, and then checking those results against actual observations.

I apply my background in numerical modelling and high-performance computing (HPC) to broader data science problems. I manage end-to-end data pipelines, wrangle terabyte-scale datasets, and use machine learning to extract actionable insights. Whether I am optimising legacy C code for supercomputers or setting up automated ETL workflows in Python and SQL, I enjoy tackling tough quantitative problems and ensuring the integrity of data-driven decisions.
